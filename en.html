The project

Methodological innovation needs an autonomous space.

Homo Methodologicus was created to build a territory where methodology is not only a tool, but a field of research, discussion and experimentation.

Social research methodology is often treated as a set of techniques serving other disciplines. Here, it becomes the focus: a place to question procedures, improve tools and make the knowledge we produce more reliable.

In a moment shaped by artificial intelligence, digitalization and new forms of data production, this reflection cannot remain peripheral. Research practices are changing quickly; method must be able to change with them, critically and collectively.

Principles

Better methods produce more responsible knowledge.

The community works on what makes research clearer, more comparable and more useful: design, tools, transparency, interpretation and social impact.

01

Methodological autonomy

Methodology as a specific object of research, not merely as a technical appendix to other fields.

02

Open experimentation

Tools, protocols and ideas are discussed, tested and improved through exchange across different perspectives.

03

Scientific responsibility

Every methodological choice makes part of reality more or less visible: it should be stated, criticized and cared for.

Taking shape

Activities in progress.

Homo Methodologicus grows through concrete initiatives open to researchers, methodologists, students and anyone interested in improving social research practices.

01

Online community

A Discord space for discussion, critical exchange, methodological questions and collective construction of better practices.

02

Digital tools for research

Development and experimentation of tools that support research design, data collection, analysis and interpretation.

03

Dialogue across disciplines and methods

An open comparison between approaches, traditions and languages, with one shared goal: improving the quality of social research.
